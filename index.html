Welcome to PAWsitive!

We are committed to bringing positive, continuous change for stray animals in our community. By merging a hopeful, compassionate approach with coherent action, we lead crucial rescue missions, provide necessary medical care, and assist lasting adoptions. From helping injured animals to finding loving homes, we try to make their lives a little better each day. And with people like you by our side, we can keep spreading kindness and hope - one small step, and one happy paw, at a time.

Helping street animals as a pawtner is more than an act of kindness it is a growing movement that strengthens our sense of community and responsibility. As students, we proudly lead by example, showing that even simple acts of compassion can create meaningful change. We encourage not only our classmates but also families, neighbors, and the wider public to care for the animals in their surroundings. When more people become involved, street animals receive the food, shelter, and medical care they desperately need, reducing suffering and making our neighborhoods safer and more peaceful. By being part of our community as a Pawsitive Pawtner, you help promote empathy, awareness, and responsible action. You become a role model who shows that every life matters. This mission teaches valuable lessons about leadership, teamwork, and compassion values that go beyond school and into real-life experiences. As more Pawsitive Pawtners join in, we create a powerful chain of kindness that inspires others, leads to more rescues, and provides more animals with the love and protection they deserve. Together, we build a community where humans and animals can thrive side by side a place where compassion is practiced, shared, and celebrated. Being a Pawsitive Pawtner means choosing to make a difference, one rescued and cared-for animal at a time.






The name PAWsitive represents our commitment to making a positive, long-term difference in the lives of stray animals in our community by promoting hope, compassion, and practical solutions.

PAWsitive

Is a project created by four proud Herons from the University of Makati. We were inspired to start this heartfelt initiative because of how close animals are in our hearts. We often see them on the streets hurt, hungry, and helpless, and instead of just feeling sad, we decided to do something about it, even in the smallest way. Our goal is simple: to help stray dogs and cats ease their starvation, protect them from harm, and find their forever homes.

MISSION

To connect compassionate animal lovers with efficient ways to express their support, ensuring that every donation directly contributes to a better life for a stray.

VISION

To envision a community where each stray animal is safe, healthy, and treated kindly; where the sufferings of stray animals are eliminated by saving, healing, and giving pets in a responsible way.

Our website serves as a dedicated platform to make the problems of strays known through educational campaigns and by providing information. Partnering with neighbourhoods by showing the progress made, what the local shelters need, and steps to give safe help to a stray, we mobilise our community members to be the change. This is the main idea behind the existence of the website - to educate and inspire people to co-exist with those in need and, in fact, to take action for a less painful future for the homeless ones whose lives are on the streets.


'Aspin' and 'puspin' are the terms which Filipinos most frequently use when talking about native or mixed-breed dogs and cats, respectively. These two are basically the Filipino equivalents of the words "mixed-breed household pet"; however, the terms have been changed to substitute the old, mostly insulting words "Askal" and "Pusakal" (meaning "street dog" and "street cat" that were used to show less contempt for these animals.


The Aspin (Asong Pinoy) is the native Filipino mixed-breed dog, which can be compared to a "village dog". These dogs are considered great because of their stamina, faithfulness, and cleverness, and they also have the genetic advantage of a hybrid, which makes them generally strong and without diseases. Although their looks can vary greatly, they most of the time have a medium size and a short coat. The word 'Aspin' was brought into common use to give way to the old and less respectful names whose purpose was to bring back the dignity of these loyal and versatile Philippine pets.

The Puspin (Pusang Pinoy) is the Filipino native mixed-breed cat, which is essentially a Domestic Shorthair type. Puspins, being very loving, friendly, and easy to care for, are also tough cats and thus can live comfortably in different surroundings. Their fur can be any colour or pattern, and like the Aspin, they are genetically healthier because they are a hybrid and therefore less likely to inherit particular health problems from purebred cats. They are the most lovable and the best companions everywhere in the Philippines.

The stray crisis in the Philippines is an increasingly alarming issue of animal welfare that has become the major consequence of overpopulation of the country's two most common dog and cat breeds: the Aspins and Puspins. The Philippine Animal Welfare Society (PAWS) has estimated the number of stray dogs and cats to be approximately 12 million based on the data from 2019 to 2022; thus, these animals are in a perpetual fight against starvation, sickness, and maltreatment. The failure to drastically reduce the population of pets through spay/neuter (kapon) procedures is at the core of this crisis, which is additionally, very much, due to the abandonment of animals increasing at such a high rate. As a result, animal rescue groups are always being carried away with their scarce resources, which leads to the often tragic fate of impoundment and euthanasia.
